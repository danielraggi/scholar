01 — About

About me

I'm a mathematician and computer scientist based in Cambridge, UK. My background is in formal reasoning, logic, and the role of representation in problem-solving.

After completing my PhD, I spent six years as a Research Associate at the University of Cambridge, working on heterogeneous reasoning and how the choice of representation shapes what we can discover.

Today I apply that same rigour across everything I do — whether that's teaching a student to write their first proof, building science-driven software, or dialling in a recipe for a perfect West Coast IPA.

03 — Software

What I build

Featured Project

Proportion Brewing Assistant

A science-first beer brewing application. Every calculation is grounded in the chemistry and physics of brewing — not rules of thumb.

Beyond recipe building, it integrates water treatment calculators, mash chemistry, and outcome prediction: what efficiency, attenuation, and flavour profile to expect given your water, grain bill, and process.

  • Recipe builder with real-time predictions
  • Water treatment & mineral adjustment
  • Mash pH and efficiency modelling
  • Outcome prediction based on process parameters
  • Lautering, boil-off, and fermentation calculators
